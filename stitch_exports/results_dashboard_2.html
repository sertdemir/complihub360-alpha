AI Summary based on your query regarding GDPR data retention policies for fintech companies operating within the EU.

Key Findings

  • 1
    Storage Limitation Principle

    Personal data must not be kept longer than necessary for the purposes for which it is processed. Fintechs must define clear retention periods. [Article 5(1)(e) GDPR]

  • 2
    Financial Records Exemptions

    While GDPR requires minimization, national laws often mandate retaining financial records for 5-10 years for tax and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) purposes, overriding immediate deletion requests. [AMLD5 Guidelines]

  • 3
    Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten)

    Users can request data deletion, but fintechs must balance this against legal obligations to retain transaction histories. [Article 17 GDPR]

Recommended Immediate Action

Review and update your Data Retention Policy document to explicitly map specific data categories to their legal retention requirements (e.g., AML vs. Marketing data).
